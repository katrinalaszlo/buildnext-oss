# Cost Intelligence

Companies can't see which customers or features are profitable. They get one bill from Anthropic at month-end with no breakdown.

**Stories:** 3 | **Evidence:** 29 quotes across 15 customers

---

## The problem

LLM providers give a total — not a breakdown. Companies fill the gap with spreadsheets (Eric Goldberg),  Google Sheets (Leo Jiang),  guesstimates (Christopher Bray),  or nothing at all (Michael Arulfo). Nobody knows their cost floor until the bill arrives.

This creates two downstream failures:
- **Runaway costs** — Julian Pscheid's Portkey overage trending up monthly. Elif Dinc's model costs tripled in 2 months with no warning.
- **Blind pricing** — Christopher Bray guesses each chat costs "maybe 2,  maybe 8" tokens. Gabriel Espindola can't tell what any customer costs them.

## Strongest signal

> "we don't have like we don't have a visibility on who brings like what in profit and we only kind of value the clients and like net revenue that they bring." — [[Elif Dinc]]

> "I just didn't want to go bankrupt from heavy users. And even with my kind of guesstimate,  I could still run into some problems if I did my math wrong." — [[Christopher Bray]]
