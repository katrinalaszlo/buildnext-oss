# Engineering Analytics

Cross-team engineering velocity measurement without manual data reconciliation.

**Stories:** 1 | **Evidence:** 10 quotes across 1 customer

---

## The problem

Engineering leaders at growing companies can't get a reliable read on team velocity across multiple squads. Each team tracks work differently — some estimate, some don't — and the only way to get a cross-team view is manual spreadsheet reconciliation that takes hours and produces stale numbers.

The consequences are real: bad staffing decisions based on wrong data, expensive tools that only one person uses, and engineering leaders becoming the "velocity police" instead of focusing on strategy.

## Strongest signal

> "We make bad staffing decisions. Last quarter we thought Team Atlas was underperforming, but it turned out they were doing tons of untracked work — code reviews, incident response, mentoring. We almost pulled their tech lead off to another team before someone caught it." — [[Alex Chen]]

> "I'd pay out of my own budget, not even go through procurement. If you could give me reliable cross-team velocity for under $500 a month, I'd sign today." — [[Alex Chen]]
